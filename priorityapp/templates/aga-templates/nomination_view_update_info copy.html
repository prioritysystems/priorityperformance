Criteria and Nominations

  • Clarity and definition of the unique talent and its official classification – the skill needs to be pinpointed, for example, fame is not a skill, but could be a result of a skill, what is the skill? Nominations need to state what the skill category is. The A.G.A will not prescribe or limit skill categories.
  • Rarity of the unique talent or skill. How rare is this skill in the country, in the world etc. The nomination needs to state the rarity of the skill and substantiate such a conclusion.
  • Relevance of the skill in terms of the needs of African people to improve their self esteem, problem solving and standard of living. Which important problem does the skill or genius address? Why is it important to solve this problem?
  • Proof of application of the skill or unique talent to resolve the problems of self esteem, lack of recognition, problem solving and standard of living of Africans. How has the nominee distinguished themselves in addressing these unique African challenges.
  • Skill replication, and the ability of the nominee to influence and train protégés to desire to possess and express the same skill. To what extent has the nominee gone out of their way to try and share their skill? This needs to be substantiated.
  • Written and contactable public testimonies of those affected by the nominee of not more than 300 words. The A.G.A will invite the public to submit testimonies. The adjudication will be done on an equal number of testimonies not less than 10 per nominee.
  • Professional or official recognition, awards and endorsements received, or justification of exclusion(from recognition) for marginalized and disadvantaged groups.
